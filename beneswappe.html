    • BeneSwappe Design

    Generating value from underutilized items


    BeneSwappe is an online clothing exchange service that lets groups of friends, coworkers, or classmates easily swap clothes that they do not want anymore. More than 15 million tons of textile are wasted each year in the US. While donating old clothing is a good option, very few of the donated clothes actually make it into the hands of someone in need.

    BeneSwappe is dedicated to generating value from items that would otherwise have been thrown away, while making it fun and easy to swap clothes with your friends.

    Design

    The Solution

    Problem: How might we generate value from unwanted clothing and items
    Users: Anyone who is cleaning out their closet
    Role: Product Designer
    Details: Personal Project, 1 month

    How it works

    Designed for any web browser, BeneSwappe lets you easily add your unwanted clothes to the site. Any member in your group can then request those items you just listed. Having a centralized location where you can list unwanted items to people you know increases the chance that the item will land in the hands of someone who wants it.

    By creating a platform that allows users to easily find a new owner for unwanted items, this extends the item's lifecycle, generating value from items that would otherwise have none.

    High-Level Process


    Process

    Understanding the user

    To understand user needs, I conducted interviews with people who have done clothing swaps before, or those who have cleaned their closets recently. I also observed users who were in the process of cleaning their closets.


    Based on my interviews and observations, I conducted a task analysis of how a user cleans their closet and sort through their clothing. This helped me identify user pain points and areas where value was lost.

    Competitive Analysis

    Because clothing exchange apps and sites do exist, I wanted to scope out the competitive landscape before starting to think about design. I looked at companies that do consignment and some form of swapping.


    From the companies that I looked at, these sites that offer the "free swap" functionality have trouble retaining traffic because the degrees of separation is too high. In interviews, I showed participants some of these sites and ask whether they would use it or not. A majority of responses answered along the lines of "it is too weird to get free items from a stranger."



    Example of companies I looked at

    Creating Personas

    I created 2 personas used throughout the design process.

    Design Decision

    Based on my interview and analysis, I decided to create a website where groups of friends, coworkers, or classmates can come together to asynchronously swap clothes.

    • Making the website group based allows users to feel more comfortable swapping clothes rather than with strangers
    • Having a location where you can list unwanted items increases the chance that the item will land in the hands of someone who wants it, thus generating value
    • Because clothes swapping is not a daily activity, I decided against a mobile app
    • A responsive website will allow users the option to use the site on their laptops or their phones

    Thoughts

    This project was based off another a nonprofit organization I started when I was in undergrad called BeneShoppe. We collected unwanted items from students and resold them on an online thrift store, then donating all profits to a charity.

    Now, 3 years later, coming up with the idea for BeneSwappe was really exciting. The most striking part was seeing the difference in my design work. Back in undergrad, I knew very little of design but I still made the logo and site anyways. Comparing the two shows how much I've grown and how much I can keep growing.

    One last quick note. I used this project as an opportunity to learn about motion design. Every prototype I created, I made sure to implement motion principles effectively. It is tough but I really enjoy it!
