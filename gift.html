    • Gift Exchange Website

    Designing a digital medium for gift exchanging


    Groups of friends, coworkers, family members, etc. get together to celebrate the holidays with gift exchange parties. However, getting everyone together before the gift exchange to draw names can prove to be challenging and time-consuming. Gift Exchange Site is designed to create efficiency in the name drawing process.

    This was a personal project where I wanted to practice digital illustrations and branding. I designed the user experience and created the visual language.



    The Solution

    Gift Exchange Site

    Problem: How might we make it easy to draw names for a gift exchange party
    Users: Anyone who is doing a gift exchange
    Role: UX/UI Designer, Visual Designer, Developer
    Details: Personal Project, 2 weeks for design

    Note: this is an ongoing project and I am currently coding the front end in HTML/CSS/JavaScript.

    High-Level Process


    I wanted to focus on creating a delightful web experience. The site is an all-inclusive gift exchange drawing service that did not focus solely on a specific holiday theme.


    Process

    Understanding the user

    I started out by talking to people who have done group gift exchanges in the past or were planning on doing one for the upcoming holidays.

    Problems include:

    • It is hard to get people in the same room, especially for a small task like drawing a name.
    • For larger groups, it is hard to keep track of who wanted to participate.
    • Sometimes people will lose the paper with the wishlist of their drawn name.

    Brainstorming and Design Alternatives

    I came up with 3 thought-out ideas and presented them to people to get their feedbacks. I asked my classmates and previous coworkers who have organized gift exchanges before.

    1. Facebook Messenger plugin that allow group chats to start a gift exchange
    2. Slack plugin that would allow companies that use Slack to easily start a gift exchange
    3. Standalone website that would allow any users to generate a link for people to sign up

    Slack option

    Design Decision

    I ended up choosing the standalone website for a couple reasons:

    1. Facebook Messenger assumes that every user will actively use Messenger. This could potentially limit the amount of users reached.
    2. Slack targets primarily companies and work. This could potentially discourage groups of friends who may not necessarily use Slack as their primary means of communication.
    3. While the website would be the most technology intensive to build, it allows the furthest reach as any user will be able to go to the site and use it.

    Design

    User Flow

    I created low fidelity wireframes to put together a user flow. I focused on the information needed on each page rather than colors or aesthetics.

    Initial user flow map

    Getting user feedback

    I put these wireframes quickly in an InVision prototype and had users go through the screens. Tasks included:

    1. As the group admin, register your group
    2. As the group admin, send the gift exchange link
    3. As the group admin, remove "Sean Duncan"
    4. As a group member, register for your group
    5. As a group member, look for your drawn name

    Final User Flows

    Register your group

    Group admins can easily register for their group.

    Register individual for group

    Users will click a link (generated by the admin) and be able to register for their group.

    Edit Wishlist

    Users can view their individual page and edit their wishlists.

    Visual Design

    Color

    While games like Secret Santa may be the most popular for gift exchanges, I did not want to have a focus on a specific holiday theme. Therefore, I chose colors that were bright and festive but were not unique to a certain holiday.

    Icons and Illustrations

    Most of the illustrations are based on the color palette and were different variations of gifts.









    Thoughts

    This was a fun exploration of visual design and interaction design. When I first set out to work on this, I didn't know that it would turn out to be website product. Because I have more experience with mobile app design, I was glad to get a chance to explore how to design in web. Learning how to work with grid layouts was especially important.
